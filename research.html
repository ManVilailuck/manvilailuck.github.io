My research focuses on systems and methods that make deployed ML systems reliable, interpretable, and aligned with institutional constraints. I approach problems that combine algorithms, infrastructure, and social context.

Research interests

  • Robust & Retrieval-Augmented ML Systems: Scalable, uncertainty-aware LLM and RAG pipelines; retrieval quality, latency, and failure-mode analysis for mission-critical applications.
  • Optimization & Stochastic Modeling for Critical Infrastructure: Optimization, simulation, and stochastic decision-making for communication networks, mobility systems, and public services under uncertainty.
  • Trustworthy Sociotechnical Systems: Fairness, accountability, and transparency in algorithmic systems operating within institutions and regulated settings.
  • Human–AI Interaction: Interfaces and oversight mechanisms for non-expert operators; human trust calibration and hybrid decision-making in operational workflows.
  • Causal Inference for Platforms & Policy: Causal modeling and experimentation to evaluate platform design, incentives, and policy interventions at scale.
  • AI Safety & Reliability: Robustness, uncertainty quantification, and error mitigation for ML systems deployed under resource and data constraints.

Approach

My methodology combines formal modeling (optimization, stochastic processes), empirical evaluation (experiments, ablation, statistical analysis), and sociotechnical critique (policy analysis, ethical frameworks). I aim to produce results that inform both research and practice.
